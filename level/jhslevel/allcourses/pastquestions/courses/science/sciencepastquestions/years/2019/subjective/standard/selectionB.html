Section A

1.(a)In an experiment, four nails 1, 2, 3 and 4 are fixed with candle wax onto a metal bar and one end of the bar is heated by means of boiling water as shown in the diagram below


A, B, C, D are thermometers inserted in holes along the bar to measure the temperatures at the various points.

(i) What is the temperature of the boiling water?

(ii) State the observations that will be made about nails 1, 2, 3 and 4

(iii) State the observations that will be made about the temperatures recorded by thermometers A, B, C and D.

(iv) What mode of heat transfer is demonstrated in the experiment

(v) State one effect of heat that is associated with the experiment

(vi) State the aim of the experiment

Suggested Answer

1. (a) (i) Temperature = 100°C or 212°F or 373K

(ii) Observations (Nails) Nails 1, 2, 3 and 4 will drop off one after the other - Nail 1 drops first, nail 2 second, nail 3 third and nail 4 fourth

(iii) Observations(Thermometers) The readings of Thermometers A,B, C and D will increase progressively till they reach 100° C -Thermometer A reaches 100° C first, B second,C third and D fourth

(iv) Mode of heat transfer - Conduction

(v) Effects of heat
1. Causes increase in temperature(as shown by the thermometer readings)
2. Causes change in state (candle wax melts - changes from solid to liquid)

(vi) Aim of experiment
1. To show that metals are good conductors of heat
2. To show that heat travels through metals
